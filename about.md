# About Me

Hi! I'm **Paul Karam**, a .NET consultant, AI trainer, and mentor with over **10 years of professional experience** building software.

My journey into software development started long before my career did. Back in high school, I discovered programming and immediately fell in love with it. Over the following years, I spent countless hours teaching myself new languages, experimenting with different platforms, building personal projects, and learning by trial and error. That curiosity eventually turned into a profession, but it has never stopped being a passion.

Today, I work across the .NET ecosystem, helping design and build scalable, maintainable software. My day-to-day work includes software architecture, backend services and APIs, performance optimization, cloud solutions with Azure, DevOps and CI/CD, mentoring fellow developers and consultants, and occasionally contributing to open-source projects. I enjoy solving complex technical problems just as much as helping others become better engineers.

Most of my experience revolves around technologies such as **C#**, **.NET**, **ASP.NET Core**, **Azure**, **SQL Server**, **PostgreSQL**, **Kubernetes**, **Azure Service Bus**, and the surrounding ecosystem that makes modern .NET development possible.

Outside the .Net world and the 0s and 1s, I am a husband, and father of a beautiful daughter! Spending quality time with the family is an important part of my life.

## Why This Blog?

Over the years, I've learned an incredible amount from the developer community. Whether it was through blog posts, open-source projects, conference talks, or discussions with other engineers, countless people have helped shape the developer I am today.

This blog is my way of giving something back.

Here, I'll share practical articles about C#, .NET, software architecture, performance, best practices, tools, and software development in general. Some posts will dive deep into technical topics, while others will document lessons I've learned through real-world experience.

Writing is also a great way to organize my own thoughts, challenge my understanding, and continue learning. If something I write helps another developer solve a problem, learn a new concept, or avoid a mistake, then this blog has done its job.

Whether you're just starting your journey with .NET or you've been building software for years, I hope you'll find something useful here.

Thanks for stopping by, and happy coding!
